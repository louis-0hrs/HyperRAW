General

What is HyperRAW?
HyperRAW is a standalone, high-performance batch converter for Blackmagic RAW (BRAW) files. It focuses on fast, reliable conversion into common production formats without requiring a full editing suite.
What’s the typical workflow?
Drag & drop one or multiple .braw files, choose an output codec and settings, then export everything in one batch. No timelines, no projects, no manual relinking.
Do I need DaVinci Resolve installed?
No. HyperRAW uses the official Blackmagic RAW SDK directly and works independently of DaVinci Resolve.

Performance & Hardware

Is a GPU required?
No — HyperRAW also runs on CPU-only systems. However, NVIDIA CUDA can significantly accelerate decoding and processing, especially at higher resolutions.
Which GPU APIs are supported?
  • CUDA (NVIDIA): supported and recommended
  • OpenCL: not supported yet (planned)
  • Multi‑GPU: not supported yet (planned)
Does HyperRAW support multiple GPUs?
Not yet. Currently HyperRAW uses a single GPU per export process. Multi‑GPU support is planned for a future release.

Formats & Output

Which input formats are supported?
Currently: Blackmagic RAW (.braw).
Which output formats are available?
Output formats depend on your system and available encoders. HyperRAW focuses on widely used, production-ready formats (e.g. common MOV-based deliverables and image sequences).
Does HyperRAW use hardware encoders?
Where applicable, HyperRAW can use system-available encoders. Availability depends on your OS, driver stack, and installed components.

Licensing & Privacy

Does HyperRAW upload, watermark, or claim rights to my media?
No. Processing happens locally. HyperRAW does not upload your footage, add watermarks, or claim any rights to your input or output files.
Does HyperRAW require an internet connection?
Only if your license type requires online validation. Conversion itself runs offline.

Roadmap

What features are planned?
Planned: OpenCL support, multi‑GPU support, additional output options, and ongoing performance improvements. Exact timelines are not fixed.
